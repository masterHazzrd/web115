Introduction

In September 1977, I entered the world amidst the scorching embrace of Tucson, AZ, launching my semi-nomadic odyssey. The early years witnessed a rhythmic dance between Davis-Monthan AFB (Tucson) and Sheppard AFB in Wichita Falls, TX. A new chapter unfurled in 1988 post my parents' divorce, steering us on another expedition that found respite in Matthews, NC. The year 1996 marked my graduation from Independence Senior High, setting the stage for a diverse career path amid studies at Central Piedmont Community College. Although initially drawn to an AAS in Automotive Systems Technology, a change of heart redirected my course three quarters into the program, leaving the degree unclaimed. Exiting the realm of academia, I returned to the familiar grounds of work life, taking on the role of a McDonald's manager. Fate had its own plan, and shortly after crossing the threshold of 22, I stepped into the realm of fatherhoodâ€”not just to one but to triplet boys. This unforeseen twist expanded my world, introducing a juggling act of parenthood to the ongoing adventure. In the quest for stability and a less tumultuous life, I invested three years in finding my bearings. Yearning for transformation, I enlisted in the Army, subjecting myself to rigorous training to emerge as a lean, mean fighting machine. During my military tenure, my professional evolution led me to become a skilled network analyst, proficient not only in constructing radio networks but also in crafting intricate computer networks. The Army, more than a mere chapter, became a transformative saga, shaping me into a multifaceted individual prepared for any challenge that lay ahead.
